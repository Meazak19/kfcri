    About

    Ms. Kirthi Jayakumar is an activist, artist, entrepreneur, and writer from Chennai, India. She is the founder of the Red Elephant Foundation, a civilian peacebuilding initiative promoting gender equality through storytelling, advocacy, and digital interventions, and fynePRINT, a feminist e-publishing imprint. She is also a member of the UN Inter-Agency Network on Youth Development’s Working Group for Gender Equality.

    Kirthi is the creator of Saahas, a web and mobile app supporting survivors of gender-based violence across 196 countries, and has spoken at global platforms including TEDx Chennai, FICCI FLO, and the UNV Partnerships Forum. Recognized internationally, she was invited to Michelle Obama’s United State of Women Summit (2016) and President Obama’s Town Hall in New Delhi (2017). Her honors include the US Presidential Services Medal, UN Online Volunteer of the Year Award (twice), the World Pulse Impact Leader Award, and the Digital Women Award for Social Impact.

    Beyond activism, Kirthi is an accomplished author and playwright, with works such as The Doodler of Dimashq, The Dove’s Lament, and plays including Frankly Speaking, HerStory, and Dolls. Her art and writing continue to inspire global audiences in peacebuilding and gender equality.
