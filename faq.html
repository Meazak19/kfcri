    Frequently Asked Questions

    01
    What is KFCRI

    KFCRI functions as an independent appointing authority, facilitating the appointment of Arbitrators from its diversified panel of experts. Our panel spans industries and regions, ensuring that every dispute is matched with the right Arbitrator who brings subject-matter competence, neutrality, and efficiency to the forefront. The appointment process is carried out in strict compliance with the Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, with customized turnaround times (TAT) and transparent communication maintained throughout.

    02
    What are the benefits of ADR over litigation?

    ADR, as an avenue for dispute resolution is generally considered to be faster, less expensive, and more flexible than traditional court proceedings.

    03
    What kinds of disputes can be settled under Arbitration?

    Arbitration could be considered as an avenue for resolving wide range of disputes, including commercial, consumer, employment and intellectual property related disputes. However, as a prerequisite for entering into arbitration, the parties shall mandatorily have a prior arbitration agreement, or an arbitration clause in the respective agreement, which is a mandatory requirement for the arbitration process to proceed.

    04
    What does it mean to be an appointing authority in Arbitration?

    When it comes to Arbitration, an appointing authority could be considered as responsible for appointing arbitrators on the occasion of the parties not being able to agree on a selection. Such role of an authority shall also ensure impartiality and competency in the arbitration proceeding. The Parties can also specify in their arbitration agreement or clause that, in the event of there arising a dispute, a particular entity shall act as the appointing authority, thereby providing expert arbitrators to facilitate fair and efficient dispute resolution. KFCRI too serves as an appointing authority, providing expert arbitrators to facilitate fair and efficient dispute resolution.
