    About KFCRI

    Kovise Foundation Conflict Resolution International

    KFCRI was established in the year 2016, under the aegis of KOVISE FOUNDATION to disseminate the required Research and Development of Conflict Resolutions thereby facilitating peace and economic balance with effective resolution of all categories of disputes in socio, economic and political arena worldwide administering Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR) Mechanism with Institutional Framework along with long term capacity building programmes seeding globally.

    Advancing Dialogue-Driven Conflict Resolution

    It has become imminent that Conflict Resolution has to be facilitated by dialogue with long term focus by way of a good communication platform and acceptable dispute resolution mechanisms adopting global standards. Apart from utilizing this member-based establishment, beneficiaries have the opportunity to benefit from the Tailor-made Rules after intensive research based on the scenario of modern economies all over the world. Thereby, KFCRI advances the development of ADR by fostering globally recognized standards, nurturing professional capacity, and reinforcing institutional credibility across jurisdictions.

    Kovise Foundation Conflict Resolution International (KFCRI)

    15th October 2016

    Establishment & Vision

    Kovise Foundation Conflict Resolution International (KFCRI), established on 15th October 2016 under the aegis of the Kovise Foundation, is a pioneering institution dedicated to advancing Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR) practices across the globe. Founded by Mrs. Surya Narmada, a Horticulturist and Agri-Arbitration Consultant, KFCRI was envisioned as a platform to make dispute resolution more accessible, inclusive, and impactful—especially in sectors like agriculture that are often underserved in traditional legal frameworks.

    Accreditation & Global Leadership

    ADR Accreditation & Expert Guidance

    KFCRI is the first institution of its kind to offer accreditation in all areas of ADR, while also functioning as a global administrative hub for managing and enhancing dispute resolution systems. The institution operates under the global guidance of Mr. V. Inbavijayan, a renowned international arbitrator with over two decades of exclusive arbitration experience. As the Global Advisor of KFCRI, Mr. Inbavijayan brings unparalleled expertise, having presided over hundreds of domestic and numerous international arbitration cases, earning widespread recognition for his impartiality and deep subject-matter knowledge.

    Ecosystem Impact

    Diversity, Inclusion & Sector Appointments

    KFCRI has consistently championed diversity and inclusion in the ADR space, with a strong emphasis on the appointment of young and women arbitrators. The institution has facilitated arbitrator appointments in thousands of banking, financial, and domestic disputes, playing a vital role in strengthening India’s ADR ecosystem.

    Specialized Education

    Training Programs & IITArb Empanelment Course

    To date, KFCRI has conducted over 40 structured training programs globally and participated in more than 100 seminars and conferences. In collaboration with the Indian Institution of Technical Arbitrators (IITArb), KFCRI launched the IITArb – Empanelment Course, a specialized training initiative for engineers to enhance their competence in arbitration. Over 200 professionals from diverse fields have been accredited through KFCRI’s programs, reflecting its commitment to interdisciplinary ADR education.

    Global Engagement

    MoUs, Roadshows & Internship Initiatives

    KFCRI has signed 25+ MoUs with ADR institutions and law schools across India and abroad, promoting global ADR exchange and collaboration. Through ADR Roadshows at over 25 institutions, including premier law schools, KFCRI has actively engaged with the next generation of legal professionals. The institution has also trained and inducted over 150 interns, many of whom have served as tribunal secretaries in more than 120 cases in the past year alone.

    Digital Resilience

    COVID-19 Resilience & Virtual Dispute Resolution

    Even during the challenges of the COVID-19 pandemic, KFCRI remained resilient and innovative, leveraging digital platforms to conduct webinars, training sessions, competitions, and conferences. It also introduced dedicated Virtual Dispute Resolution (VDR) rules, ensuring efficient and accessible proceedings in a digital-first world. As an appointing authority, KFCRI maintains a sector-wise panel of arbitrators with deep subject-matter expertise. The institution is also active in mediation, offering both training and actual mediation services, further expanding its impact in the ADR ecosystem.

    2025 Flagship Event

    Global South ADR Thirunaal / Day

    In 2025, KFCRI proudly presents the 1st Congress of ADR Practitioners and Institutions in Global South Countries through the Global South ADR Thirunaal / Day, a 24-hour online event that underscores its commitment to global collaboration, innovation, and leadership in ADR.
