    This position is designed for dynamic legal professionals

    At KFCRI, Case Counsels play a pivotal role in ensuring the smooth functioning of our institutional processes. They are entrusted with responsibilities that span case management, research, stakeholder engagement, and representation, thereby contributing to both the operational and academic growth of the Centre. This position is designed for dynamic legal professionals who are committed to advancing ADR practice and strengthening KFCRI’s institutional presence.

    Key Responsibilities :

    01

    Research Insights

    Undertake in-depth research in the field of ADR and contribute to content creation, publications, and knowledge resources.

    02

    Process Oversight

    Oversee the administration of ADR proceedings, including the review and due diligence of institutional, regulatory, statutory, and legal aspects of each case to ensure procedural compliance and efficiency.

    03

    Global Networking

    Assist in building partnerships and exploring networking opportunities both domestically and across international regions.

    04

    Brand Representation

    Represent KFCRI at conferences, trainings, seminars, and other professional forums, strengthening the Centre’s visibility and credibility.

    05

    Process Coordination

    Coordinate with parties, and other stakeholders to facilitate smooth communication and case progression.

    06

    Capacity Building

    Contribute to institutional programmes, initiatives, and special projects as assigned, supporting KFCRI’s mission of capacity building.

    Basic Qualifications for Case Counsels

    Domestic

    • Bachelor’s Degree in Law from a University/College recognized by the Bar Council of India
    • Enrolment as an Advocate with a State Bar Council.
    • Prior exposure to the field of ADR will be considered an advantage.
    • Strong drafting, writing, and comprehension skills.
    • Fluency in English.
    • Basic proficiency in computer applications (MS Office)

    International

    • Bachelor’s Degree in Law from a recognized University/College
    • Minimum of 1 year of professional experience.
    • Prior experience with ADR institutions, law firms handling dispute resolution, or international arbitration will be considered an advantage.
    • Fluency in English and the candidate’s respective regional language(s).
    • Strong drafting, writing, and comprehension skills.
    • Ability to multitask and manage responsibilities effectively.
    • Basic proficiency in computer applications.
